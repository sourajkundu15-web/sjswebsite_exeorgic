Description:The Hand Gesture Tank is a robot that operates using an accelerometer to detect and record hand movements. Description:The PULL IT – Block-and-Tackle Pulley System demonstrates the principle of mechanical advantage using a combination of 3 fixed pulleys and 2 movable pulleys. This arrangement provides a mechanical advantage of 5, meaning it can lift a load five times greater than the effort applied. By distributing the weight across multiple ropes, the system reduces the required effort while making lifting more efficient. This project highlights the practical application of simple machines in reducing human effort and showcases how pulleys are widely used in construction, transport, and everyday lifting mechanisms.

Description:The Spotlight on Single Slit Diffraction project demonstrates the visualization of a diffraction pattern formed when a monochromatic laser source passes through a narrow single slit. The light waves spread out and interfere with one another, producing a distinct pattern of bright and dark fringes on a screen. At the center lies the central maximum, which is the brightest and widest fringe, surrounded by alternating minima and secondary maxima. This experiment highlights the wave nature of light and provides a clear understanding of fundamental optical phenomena.

Description:This project demonstrates the working of hydraulic cranes, which operate on Pascal's Law. According to this principle, pressure applied to a confined fluid is transmitted equally in all directions, allowing small forces to generate larger lifting forces. By applying pressure within a closed system, the crane can lift heavy loads efficiently. The model incorporates components such as pulleys, wheels, and axles to illustrate how hydraulic systems amplify force, making them essential in real-world construction and material-handling applications.

Women in STEAM: Powering the Future with Robotics & AI

From labs to space, from codes to canvases — women have been at the heart of every leap in STEAM. EXOERGIC 2025 celebrates their courage, brilliance, and groundbreaking achievements, honoring their unstoppable drive to inspire tomorrow’s innovators and leaders.

Global Women Achievers

Meet some extraordinary women who broke barriers, challenged norms, and left a lasting mark on the world of STEAM:

Marie Curie was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and the only person ever to win Nobel Prizes in two sciences. She discovered polonium and radium, pioneering research that transformed physics and medicine.

Lise Meitner was a physicist who co-discovered nuclear fission in 1939. Despite facing discrimination, she made groundbreaking contributions and is remembered as the “mother of nuclear fission.”

Sofia Kovalevskaya was the first woman professor of mathematics in Northern Europe. She advanced differential equations and mathematical physics, breaking barriers for women in academia.

Grace Hopper, U.S. Navy Rear Admiral, created the first compiler in 1952 and helped develop COBOL. “Amazing Grace” pioneered modern programming.

Shakuntala Devi, the “Human Computer,” stunned the world with extraordinary mental calculations, setting records in 1977 and 1982. She inspired generations with her books and talent.

Karen Spärck Jones introduced Inverse Document Frequency (IDF) in 1972, the foundation of modern search engines. Her work shaped internet search and information retrieval.

Fumiko Yonezawa advanced condensed matter theory and amorphous semiconductors. In 1996, she became the first woman president of the Physical Society of Japan.

Fei-Fei Li co-created ImageNet in 2007, revolutionizing AI and machine learning. She is a Stanford professor and advocate for ethical AI in healthcare.

Timnit Gebru is a leading AI researcher on algorithmic bias and ethics. She co-founded Black in AI and advocates for responsible AI development.

Grazia Deledda, Italian novelist, won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1926. Her works explored women’s struggles, tradition, and modernity.

Hayat Sindi is a pioneering biotechnologist, co-founder of Diagnostics for All, and the first Saudi woman with a Ph.D. in biotechnology from Cambridge. She inspires women in science globally.

Elanor Huntington is an Australian physicist and engineer specializing in quantum computing and systems engineering. A strong advocate for diversity in STEM, she has led major academic institutions.

Pinaree Sanpitak is a Thai contemporary artist exploring femininity, identity, and the female body. She is among Thailand’s most influential artists, blending creativity with global STEAM movements.
